Carbon Emissions & Sustainable Goals in Coal Mines

Understanding and Managing Carbon Footprints for a Sustainable Future

What is Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHGs) produced directly or indirectly by human activities, expressed in equivalent tons of carbon dioxide (CO2e).

Sources of Carbon Emissions in Coal Mining

Key Contributors

  • Fugitive emissions from coal seams
  • Combustion of fuels in mining equipment
  • Electricity consumption during mining operations
  • Transportation of coal
  • Land-use changes and methane leaks

Sustainable Goals for Reducing Carbon Footprints

Here are advanced strategies coal mines can adopt to align with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs):

Strategies

  • Methane Capture & Utilization for Power Generation
  • Carbon Sequestration through Biochar, an Innovative Soil Amendment
  • Post-Mining Carbon Sinks using Native Vegetation & Biodiversity Programs
  • Clean Coal Technologies for Increased Efficiency
  • Solar, Wind, and Hydropower Integration for Offsetting Emissions
  • Hybrid Diesel-Electric Mining Vehicles to Reduce Fuel Costs
  • Efficient Water Management to Minimize Carbon Impact of Mining Processes
  • Mine Tailings as Carbon Capture Sites through Enhanced Mineralization

Cost-Saving & Carbon Reduction Innovations

Innovative Approaches

  • Using AI & Machine Learning for Predictive Maintenance of Mining Equipment, Reducing Energy Use
  • Digitizing Mine Operations to Increase Efficiency and Decrease Waste
  • Implementing Blockchain for Carbon Credit Tracking and Trading
  • Hydrogen Fuel Cells for Emission-Free Mining Vehicles
  • Utilizing Circular Economy Principles: Reprocessing Mine Waste for Valuable Resources
